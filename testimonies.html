Voices of Kazanchis

Our truth, our pain, our power.

Solomon Tesfaye "Engish"

"I was born in Kazanchis. I lived and died with it. We were not just evicted, we were erased."

Martyred: September 28, 2024 | Age: 42 | Loved for his wit, loyalty, and poetry.

Surafel

"My mother’s bones are buried here. How can you ask me to leave this land?"

Last seen alive: October 2, 2024 | Remembered for resisting demolition peacefully.

Unnamed Aunt

"I cried to the camera, and they dragged me out the next day. I’ve slept on church floors since."

Evicted from Woreda Hall for speaking out. Survivor of forced demolition.

Getaneh Bezabih

"We sang on our porches. We held hands during funerals. They didn’t just steal homes—they stole community."

Beloved elder. One of the last voices preserving oral history of Kazanchis.
